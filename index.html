About the Crop:

The Mango (Mangifera indica) Langra variety starts yielding fruit in 3 to 4 years from grafted saplings, or 5 to 7 years from seed-grown plants. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH of 5.5 to 7.5. Each acre can produce 10 to 15 tons of fruit annually, with water needs around 1,000 to 1,200 mm per year, adjusted for local rainfall. Costs, including planting, fertilizers, and labor, range from ₹60,000 to ₹90,000 per acre. With market prices averaging ₹40 to ₹60 per kg, net profit can range from ₹3,00,000 to ₹6,00,000 per acre, depending on yield and market conditions. Proper management ensures optimal fruit production and profitability.

  • Soil Composition: Sandy, Loamy and Clayey Soil with Ph 5.5 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 1000-1200 mm per acre annually
  • Potential Dangers: Mango Leafhopper, Oidium mangiferae, Xanthomonas campestris
  • Fertilizers: Single Super Phosphate, Urea (46-0-0), Magnesium Sulfate
  • Pesticides: Imidacloprid, Lambda-Cyhalothrin, Malathion
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹2,65,000 to ₹8,20,000 per acre annually
